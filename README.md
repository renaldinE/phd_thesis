# Thesis outline

This file serves as a guideline for structuring the PhD thesis with
temporary title:

**"Production of radiolanthanides for nuclear medicine applications"**

The thesis will follow a **cumulative thesis structure** with a limited
number of chapters:

1. Introduction
2. One chapter for each paper
3. Conclusions and outlook

# Chapter 1 --- Introduction

scientific background necessary to understand the work presented in the thesis.

## 1.1 Cancer burden and motivation

Start with global cancer incidence and mortality statistics.\
Explain limitations of conventional treatments such as:

- surgery
- chemotherapy
- external beam radiotherapy
- immunotherapy

Motivate the need for **targeted radionuclide therapy** and nuclear
medicine approaches.

## 1.2 Nuclear medicine

Introduce the basic principles of nuclear medicine.

Topics to discuss:

- concept of radiopharmaceuticals
- targeting vectors (antibodies, peptides, small molecules)
- differences between diagnostic and therapeutic nuclear medicine

### 1.2.1 Diagnostic nuclear medicine

Explain:

- PET (Positron Emission Tomography)
- SPECT (Single Photon Emission Computed Tomography)

Introduce common radionuclides:

- F-18
- Tc-99m
- Ga-68

Briefly explain the detection principles.

### 1.2.2 Therapeutic nuclear medicine

Explain targeted radionuclide therapy and radiation types used:

- $\beta$⁻ emitters
- $\alpha$ emitters
- Auger electron emitters

Provide examples of established therapeutic radionuclides:

- Lu-177
- Y-90
- I-131

### 1.2.3 Theranostics

Explain the theranostic concept where diagnostic and therapeutic
radionuclides form chemically matched pairs.

Examples:

- Ga-68 / Lu-177
- Terbium case with Tb-149, -152, -155, -161
- potential lanthanide-based pairs

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 1.3 Radiolanthanides in nuclear medicine

Explain why lanthanides are attractive radionuclides.

Topics:

- similar chemistry across lanthanides
- compatibility with chelators such as DOTA
- presence of both diagnostic and therapeutic isotopes
- theranostic potential

Introduce the radiolanthanides investigated in this thesis:

- **Thulium-167**
- **Holmium-161**
- **Holmium-166**

Discuss their decay properties and potential applications. Make a table

## 1.4 Production of medical radionuclides

Provide a general overview of radionuclide production.

### 1.4.1 Nuclear reactions

Explain basic reaction mechanisms, show nuclide chart and the use of charged particles or neutrons to produce specific radionuclides.

### 1.4.2 Radionuclide production workflow

Describe the interconnected steps required to produce a medical
radionuclide:

- target preparation (targetry)
- irradiation
- radiochemical separation
- quality control

### 1.4.3 Cross‑section measurements

Explain:

- excitation functions
- stacked foil technique
- activation equation
- importance for yield prediction

### 1.4.4 Liquid column chromatography

Explain:

- fundamentals
- why using chromatography for radionuclide production
- extraction and ion exchange chromatography
- weight distribution coefficients
